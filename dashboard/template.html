The honest basics

What this lab can and cannot tell you

Backtests describe the past, not the future

A strategy that beat buy-and-hold over the last year did so in one specific market. The next year is a different market. Professionals treat a good backtest as a reason to keep testing — out-of-sample, across assets, across time windows — never as proof.

Most strategies do not beat simply holding

After fees, spreads, and mistimed entries, most rule-based retail strategies underperform doing nothing. When a backtest here shows a strategy winning, check its maximum drawdown too: a higher return with a much deeper drawdown is not a better strategy, it is a riskier one.

Indicators describe, they don't predict

"Oversold" (RSI below 30) means price fell hard recently — it does not mean price must bounce. Markets that crash get more oversold. Every reading in the Market Scan is a description of what already happened.

Risk management is the only part you control

You cannot control returns. You can control position size (never risk more than a small fraction on one idea), diversification, and having a written plan for when you are wrong. That is what separates investing from gambling.

The safe path to ever trading live

1) Learn the mechanics with paper trading. 2) Backtest ideas and study drawdowns. 3) Validate out-of-sample. 4) If ever going live: tiny size, hard stop-losses, human confirmation on every order, and money you can afford to lose entirely.
